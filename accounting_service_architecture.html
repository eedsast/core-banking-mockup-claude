Accounting Service — Internal Architecture

Tenant-scoped · Double-entry · Immutable ledger

Accounting service
All zones are tenant-isolated · append-only journal is the source of truth
Setup & definition
Config-time · defines the accounting structure · low-write, high-read
GL product definition
Account type, normal balance, classification (asset, liability, equity, income, expense)
Chart of accounts
Hierarchy, account codes, segment structure, parent-child mapping
GL account opening
Currency assignment, branch binding, opening balance, status
Accounting setup
Rounding mode & scale, fiscal year & period definition, period-close rules, suspense / FX gain-loss accounts, posting rules
business events trigger GL operations
GL account operations
Runtime · triggered by scheduled jobs or domain service events · each operation produces journal entries
FX revaluation
Unrealised P&L on open FX positions, driven by tenant exchange rates
Amortisation
Effective interest rate method, fee amortisation over instrument life
Depreciation
Straight-line, declining balance, units of production methods
GL account info
Current balance, period balance, account statement, status enquiry
all operations post here as immutable entries
Journal & transaction ledger
Immutable · append-only · no updates, no deletes · corrections via reversal entries only
Journal entry
Header: date, narrative, source system, reference, status (posted / reversed)
Transaction lines
One leg per account, debit/credit flag, amount in transaction currency and base currency
Period & closing
Period lock, trial balance snapshot, closing entries, carry-forward
Note —Accruals are not handled here. Domain services (e.g. LMS for loan accruals) calculate and initiate accruals within their own context. The Accounting service receives the result as a standard GL journal entry. This keeps accrual business logic co-located with the domain that owns it.
