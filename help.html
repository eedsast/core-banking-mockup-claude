Loan Management
Loan Products

A Loan Product is the master template that defines the financial and operational parameters for a class of loans. Every loan account opened on the platform is bound to exactly one Loan Product, and inherits its rules at origination.

Key parameters defined at the product level include the interest rate structure, repayment type, allowed currencies, tenor limits, fee schedule, and accounting treatment. Individual loan accounts may override some parameters within bounds set by the product.

Loan Management › Loan Products
Accounting Method

The Accounting Method controls how interest is recognised and posted to the General Ledger throughout the life of a loan. It determines the timing and amount of each accrual entry.

Available Methods

MethodDescriptionTypical use
Accrual Interest is accrued daily and posted to an accrued interest receivable account. Revenue is recognised as it is earned, regardless of cash receipt. Standard retail and SME lending, IFRS 9 compliant portfolios
Cash Basis Interest is recognised only when cash is received. No accrual entries are posted in advance. Non-performing loans, stage 3 assets, high-credit-risk portfolios
EIR (Effective Interest Rate) Interest and fee income are spread over the loan life using the constant-yield (effective interest rate) method. Origination fees and direct costs are included in the EIR calculation. IFRS 9 / IFRS 15 compliant recognition; mortgage and structured products
Important Once a loan account is opened, the Accounting Method cannot be changed. Changes to the method on a Loan Product apply only to accounts opened after the change is saved.

How Accrual Works

Each night the EoD batch calculates the interest accrued for the day using the formula below, then posts a debit to Accrued Interest Receivable and a credit to Interest Income.

Daily accrual = Outstanding principal × Annual rate ÷ Day count basis -- Example (Actual/365, 10 % p.a., balance 10 000): -- Daily accrual = 10 000 × 0.10 / 365 = 2.7397

EIR Method Detail

Under the EIR method the system calculates an internal rate of return that equates the present value of all expected cash flows (including fees) to the initial loan amount. This rate is then applied to the amortised cost balance each period to determine income. See Effective Interest Rate (EIR) for the full calculation.

Regulatory note IFRS 9 requires the EIR method for loans measured at amortised cost. Using Cash Basis for performing loans may result in under-recognition of income and require restatement.

Day Count Convention

The Day Count Convention defines how the number of days in an interest period and the number of days in a year are calculated. Different conventions produce different accrual amounts even with the same nominal rate.

ConventionDays in periodDays in yearNotes
Actual / 365Actual calendar days365 (fixed)Most common in retail banking; leap years use 365 denominator
Actual / 360Actual calendar days360 (fixed)Common in money markets and US commercial lending; produces slightly higher interest
Actual / ActualActual calendar days365 or 366 (leap year)Precise; used in government bonds and IFRS-compliant instruments
30 / 360Each month treated as 30 days360Simplifies calculation; common in fixed-income securities and some mortgage markets
30E / 360European variant of 30/360360End-of-month dates adjusted to 30; used in Eurobonds
Tip Actual/365 and Actual/360 give different annual yields for the same nominal rate. A loan at 10 % Actual/360 is effectively ~10.14 % Actual/365. Always confirm the convention with your product team before launch.

Interest Type

Determines whether the interest rate applied to a loan is fixed throughout its life or can vary in line with a reference rate.

TypeDescription
FixedThe interest rate is set at origination and does not change for the loan term. Provides payment certainty for the borrower.
VariableThe rate floats against a reference index (e.g. EURIBOR, SOFR, central bank rate). Repricing occurs on a defined schedule.
MixedFixed for an initial period (e.g. 24 months), then converts to variable for the remainder of the term.

Repayment Plan

The Repayment Plan (also called Amortisation Schedule) defines how the outstanding principal and interest are distributed across instalments over the loan term.

Plan TypeDescription
Annuity (French)Equal total instalments throughout the term. Each payment contains a growing principal component and a shrinking interest component.
Linear (German)Equal principal instalments. The interest portion decreases each period as the outstanding balance reduces. Total payments are higher initially.
BulletInterest-only payments during the term; full principal repaid at maturity. Common in short-term and bridge financing.
BalloonRegular instalments (annuity or linear) with a large lump-sum payment at maturity covering the residual principal.
IrregularCustom schedule with manually defined due dates and amounts. Used for restructured or bespoke facilities.

Interest Accrual

Interest Accrual defines the rules and frequency for recognising interest income between instalment due dates. Accrual runs as part of the nightly EoD batch.

Accrual Frequency

Defines how often accrual entries are posted to the GL. Options are: Daily (one entry per calendar day), Monthly (one entry on the last day of each month), or On Due Date (accrual posted only when an instalment falls due).

Suspended Accrual

When a loan enters a non-performing state (typically 90+ days past due), accrual is suspended. Interest continues to accrue off-balance-sheet in a memorandum account and is recognised as income only upon collection.

EoD Batch Accrual is calculated and posted automatically each night. Manual adjustment is possible via a journal override with appropriate authorisation.

Loan Management
Collections

A Collection Definition specifies the rules for triggering and processing collections when a loan instalment becomes overdue.

Collection Trigger

Collections can be triggered automatically by the EoD batch when a due date passes without full payment, or manually by an authorised user. The trigger threshold (number of days past due) is set per product.

Collection Allocation

When a collection payment is received, the platform allocates it in the following default order: Penalty interest → Overdue interest → Overdue principal → Current interest → Current principal. The allocation order can be customised per product.

Penalty Definitions

Penalty Definitions control the rate and basis of charges applied when a borrower misses a scheduled payment or repays early.

Penalty TypeDescription
Late Payment PenaltyA daily rate applied to the overdue amount for each calendar day past the due date. Expressed as an annual percentage rate.
Early Repayment FeeA one-time charge calculated on the prepaid principal, compensating the bank for lost future interest income. May be a flat amount or a percentage.
Non-utilisation FeeApplied to revolving and overdraft facilities where the borrower does not draw the committed limit. Charged on the undrawn portion.

Effective Interest Rate (EIR)

The Effective Interest Rate (EIR) is the rate that exactly discounts the estimated future cash flows of a loan back to its net carrying amount at initial recognition. Under IFRS 9 it is the basis for interest income recognition on amortised-cost assets.

What is included in the EIR

  • Nominal contractual interest
  • Origination fees charged to the borrower (e.g. processing fee, commitment fee)
  • Direct incremental origination costs (e.g. broker commissions)
  • Any discount or premium on the principal

Calculation Approach

The system solves for the internal rate of return r in the following equation, where CFt is the cash flow at time t and n is the number of periods:

Net proceeds = Σ [ CFt / (1 + r)t ] for t = 1 to n -- Net proceeds = Loan amount – upfront fees + direct costs -- CF = scheduled principal + interest payments
Preliminary vs Final EIR A preliminary EIR is calculated at application stage using the expected repayment schedule. The final EIR is locked at loan opening once all fees are confirmed and may differ slightly from the preliminary figure. Both are visible in the loan account detail view.

Decision Engine
Rule Groups

A Rule Group is a named container that bundles together a set of related rules evaluated as a unit. Typically one Rule Group exists per loan product or credit use-case (e.g. "Consumer Loan Rules", "Mortgage Rules").

When an application is submitted to the Decision Engine, the calling system specifies which Rule Group to execute. All active rules within the group are evaluated in priority order.

Decision Engine
Rules

A Rule is a single evaluable condition that contributes to the overall credit decision. Rules are processed in ascending priority order within their group.

Rule Types

TypeDescriptionEffect
Filter A hard pass/fail gate. If the condition is not met, the application is immediately declined and no further rules are evaluated. Decline or continue
Score A weighted condition that contributes a numeric score. The total score is compared against Rule Thresholds to determine the outcome. Adds to total score

Decision Engine
Rule Thresholds

Rule Thresholds map score ranges to decision outcomes. After all Score rules are evaluated and a total score is calculated, it is matched against the active thresholds for the Rule Group to determine the final outcome.

Example Score 750–999 → APPROVED · Score 600–749 → REFERRED · Score 0–599 → DECLINED

Thresholds must be contiguous (no gaps) and non-overlapping. The system validates this constraint when thresholds are saved.

Outcome Codes

Outcome Codes are the standardised responses returned by the Decision Engine after evaluating an application. They are returned in the API response and drive downstream workflow routing.

CodeMeaning
APPROVEDApplication meets all criteria. Loan opening may proceed automatically.
REFERREDApplication requires manual underwriter review before a decision is made.
DECLINEDApplication does not meet minimum criteria. Loan opening is rejected.
CONDITIONALLY_APPROVEDApproved subject to additional conditions (e.g. collateral, guarantor).
PENDING_DOCSDecision deferred pending receipt of required documentation.
ERRORThe engine could not complete evaluation due to a system or data error. See execution logs.

Property Mappings

Property Mappings define how fields in the incoming API request are translated into internal engine properties used by rules. This decouples the rule logic from the API contract — if the source field name changes, only the mapping needs updating, not the rules themselves.

FieldDescription
Source FieldThe JSON path in the incoming API payload (e.g. applicant.monthlyIncome).
Engine PropertyThe internal constant name used in rule conditions (e.g. NET_MONTHLY_INCOME).
TypeData type used for comparison: Decimal, Integer, or String.
RequiredIf true, the engine returns an ERROR outcome when the field is absent from the request.

Chart of Accounts

The Chart of Accounts (CoA) is the hierarchical structure of all GL accounts used by the organisation. It defines account codes, parent-child relationships, and the segment structure used for reporting.

Tenant isolation Each tenant maintains its own Chart of Accounts. Account codes from one tenant cannot be referenced in postings for another tenant.

Journal Entry

A Journal Entry is an immutable double-entry record in the GL. Every financial event in the system (loan disbursement, interest accrual, fee collection, etc.) generates one or more journal entries.

Structure

Each journal entry has a header (date, narrative, source system, reference, status) and two or more lines (account, debit/credit flag, amount in transaction currency, amount in base currency). The sum of debits must equal the sum of credits.

Corrections

Journal entries are append-only and cannot be deleted or modified. Corrections are made by posting a reversal entry (equal and opposite), followed by the corrected entry. Both the original and the reversal remain visible in the audit trail.

↑ Back to top
Accounting
Period Close

Period Close locks an accounting period against new postings, generates a trial balance snapshot, and prepares closing entries that carry balances forward to the next period.

Irreversible action Once a period is locked it cannot be reopened without a system administrator override. Ensure all accruals, adjustments, and reconciliations are complete before closing.

Glossary — All Terms A–Z

Quick definitions for all key terms used across the platform.

TermDefinition
AccrualThe daily recognition of interest income earned but not yet received in cash.
Amortised CostThe amount at which a financial asset is measured after deducting principal repayments and adding/deducting cumulative amortisation using the EIR method.
AnnuityA series of equal periodic payments combining principal and interest such that the loan is fully repaid at maturity.
Balloon PaymentA large lump-sum payment due at maturity representing the residual principal balance after regular instalment payments.
Bullet LoanA loan where only interest is paid periodically and the full principal is repaid at maturity.
Day Count ConventionThe rule used to calculate the number of days in an interest period and the number of days in a year for interest accrual purposes.
DTIDebt-to-Income ratio. Total monthly debt obligations divided by gross monthly income. Used as a credit filter in the Decision Engine.
EIREffective Interest Rate. The rate that equates the present value of all expected cash flows to the net carrying amount at initial recognition. See EIR section.
EoDEnd of Day. The nightly batch processing window during which accruals, penalty calculations, balance updates, and reporting extracts are generated.
Filter RuleA hard go/no-go rule in the Decision Engine that immediately declines an application if the condition is not met.
GLGeneral Ledger. The master record of all financial transactions, organised by account.
IFRS 9International Financial Reporting Standard 9. Governs the classification, measurement, and impairment of financial instruments.
LTVLoan-to-Value ratio. Loan amount divided by the appraised value of collateral. Used as a credit filter for secured products.
Non-performing Loan (NPL)A loan where the borrower is 90 or more days past due on a scheduled payment. Accrual is typically suspended on NPLs.
Outcome CodeThe standardised result returned by the Decision Engine after evaluating a credit application. See Outcome Codes.
PeriodAn accounting period, typically a calendar month, during which transactions are recorded and at the end of which the GL is closed.
Property MappingThe configuration that maps an API request field to a Decision Engine internal property. See Property Mappings.
RediscountingThe accounting process of reversing previously recognised unearned income, used in certain accrual adjustments.
ReferredA Decision Engine outcome indicating that the application requires manual underwriter review.
Rule GroupA named set of credit rules evaluated together for a specific product or use-case. See Rule Groups.
Rule ThresholdA score band mapped to a decision outcome in the Decision Engine. See Rule Thresholds.
Score RuleA weighted rule in the Decision Engine that contributes a numeric score to the total, which is then compared against thresholds.
TenantAn isolated organisational entity within the platform. Each tenant has its own data, configuration, and GL.
